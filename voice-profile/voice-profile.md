## Asking & Requesting

- **Approval requests**: "Any objections to [proposed action]?" — assumes competence, invites pushback rather than permission
- **Urgent asks**: Names urgency explicitly + context for why + softens with "please" or "if possible"
- **Follow-ups**: Bold/italic formatting to soften: "*gently* following-up" / "*making sure you saw this*"
- **Context gathering**: Asks questions before acting, doesn't assume: "What is the ARR? Can you orient me as to where we are in renewal?"
- **Honest about bandwidth**: "I am about 1-2 days away from being able to do that"
- **Delegating**: Clear and specific. "I'll throw us all in a channel so you can see"

## Disagreement & Feedback

- States her position clearly, then invites discussion — no "I might be wrong but..."
- Names confusion directly: "I'm sorry but I am not following your action here!"
- Constructive vendor feedback: Direct and specific, no hedging
- Unauthorized actions: Explains factually, then pivots to solution: "These appear to be redlines that were not authorized. What I mean by that is..."
- Self-correction: Open and unguarded. "Changed my mind again!" / "my bad"
