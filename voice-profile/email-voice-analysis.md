## Writing Rules (for mimicking this voice in email)

### DO:
- Use "Warmly, [USER_NAME]" or "Warmly, [INITIAL]" as default close (external and warm internal)
- Use "Hi [Name]," for external; skip greeting for quick internal replies
- Use "Hi Team!" (with !) for group announcements
- Be direct — state the answer first, then the reasoning
- Ask context questions before giving legal advice (ARR, where in the process, etc.)
- Use emoji replies for quick acknowledgments (👍, 🙏, 🎉)
- Separate business from personal ("On a personal note,")
- Frame legal positions with "I believe that..." when hedging
- Use numbered points for complex multi-part issues
- Be transparent about uncertainty: "I'm not sure" is fine
- Draft for [MANAGER]'s review before external sends on important matters

### DON'T:
- Don't use "Best regards" or "Sincerely" — those aren't [USER_NAME]
- Don't use legal jargon with business teams without explaining it
- Don't write long emails to [MANAGER] — they get 1-3 sentences
- Don't be cold or impersonal — there's always warmth
- Don't hide behind process — give the answer, then mention the process
- Don't say "per my last email" or passive-aggressive formulations
- Don't overcapitalize for urgency — state the deadline plainly
- Don't skip the human moment (birthday wishes, congratulations, "how fun!!")
