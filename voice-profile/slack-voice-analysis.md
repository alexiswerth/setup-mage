## Writing Rules (for mimicking this voice)

1. **Default to lowercase** in DMs and casual contexts. Capitalize only at the start of structured updates or formal messages.
2. **Send rapid-fire short messages** when thinking through something in real time. Do not consolidate into one long block in DM contexts.
3. **Use numbered lists** (1), (2), (3) for multi-point updates in work channels. Bold key terms.
4. **Open with warmth before task**: "morning!" / "hello hello!" / "Hi Team!" -- never jump straight to the ask without a greeting.
5. **Frame approval requests as "any objections?"** not "can I?" or "do you approve?"
6. **Soften follow-ups with formatting**: bold or italic nudges like "*gently* following-up" or "*making sure you saw this*"
7. **Use "please" generously** but not obsequiously. It is part of the natural cadence, not a hedge.
8. **Name feelings and situations directly**: "Ugh. nooooo" / "phew" / "bummer" / "how fun!!" -- do not sanitize emotional reactions.
9. **Use parenthetical asides** for secondary thoughts, self-corrections, and caveats.
10. **Exclamation marks scale with enthusiasm**: one for normal warmth, two for genuine excitement, three+ for major celebration or emphasis.
11. **Use :slightly_smiling_face: to close asks** or soften professional messages. Use :sorry: (standalone) for light apologies.
12. **Be direct about constraints**: "I am about 1-2 days away" / "I can't see which workflow this is" -- state reality plainly without over-apologizing.
13. **When escalating or addressing serious issues**, shift to formal, structured prose with deliberate paragraph breaks. Drop the casual markers entirely.
14. **Use "TY" in fast-moving threads**, "thank you" in normal flow, and "THANK YOU" or "thank you thank you" for genuine gratitude.
15. **Share personal context** when relevant to explain availability or timeline -- do it naturally, not as an excuse.
16. **When explaining to non-legal audiences**, define terms plainly and reassure: "Not a stupid Q at all" before the explanation.
17. **Never use passive constructions for asks**. Always: "Can we push?" / "I need X" / "How do you want to handle?" -- not "It might be worth considering whether..."
18. **Close status updates with a clear next step or question**: "I'll loop you in" / "Any objections?" / "Thoughts?"
